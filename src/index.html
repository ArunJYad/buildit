What do we do?

Buildit helps companies realise the value of the business and IT collaborating with each other. We do this by:

  • Identifying business metrics that are applicable to IT, and identifying how to work with them
  • Developing resilient, repeatable, redundant processes that predictably deliver working software to customers
  • Using the desired end state of the business to identify where and how to change how software is developed, tested, and supported
  • Introducing new ways of thinking that prioritise customers

What this involves:

  • Thinking about problems from the customer’s viewpoint
  • Breaking down barriers between the business and IT, and within IT itself, that stop people from working together
  • Looking at business and IT goals as a system, rather than a collection of independent, isolated components
  • Using metrics as a means of removing ego and opinion from decision-making
  • Reducing waste in current processes
  • Creating new processes

How this happens

  • Designing application to be less dependent on expensive commercial software
  • Decoupling applications
  • Automating delivery pipelines
  • Reducing handoffs between different silos
  • Moving to newer technologies, with a greater emphasis on self-healing, self-scaling, systems

What do we do? (Alt)

We recognise digital transformation is far more than the tech. It is an activity that addresses the complex, and that complexity has no simple answers.

Buildit works with its partner organisations to help clients understand and make sense of this complexity, and to make change.

Involving:

  • Work with clients to identify where and how to change, and how to develop, test, and support solutions
  • Identify the right business and IT metrics and how to work with them
  • Develop resilient, repeatable, redundant processes that consistently deliver working software to customers
  • Introduce new ways of thinking that prioritise customers and their experiences

Meaning:

  • Thinking about problems from the customer’s viewpoint
  • Breaking down internal client barriers that stop people from working together
  • Seeing the entire organisation as a system, rather than a collection of independent, isolated parts
  • Using metrics as a way to allow data-driven decision-making
  • Reducing waste in current processes
  • Creating new processes

Proving:

  • Application design with reduced dependence on costly commercial software
  • Infrastructure agnostic decoupled application solutions
  • Automated delivery pipelines
  • Reduced handoffs between different parts of the organisation
  • The value of shifting to newer technologies, with emphasis on security, scalability and resilience
